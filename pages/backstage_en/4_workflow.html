Overview of the Vaulted Harmonies production workflow.

The creation of Vaulted Harmonies combined historical research, 3D reconstruction, room acoustics simulation, and audiovisual production workflows. The visual reconstructions of Notre-Dame across different historical periods were created using Blender and Unreal Engine, based on architectural and archaeological hypotheses developed by the research team.

The corresponding acoustics were reconstructed from room impulse responses simulated with CATT-Acoustic models. These simulations were then used to generate dynamic spatial audio renderings in Reaper, allowing the acoustics to evolve continuously as the virtual camera moved through the cathedral.

Visual and audio teams worked jointly throughout the production process. Camera trajectories, scene composition, and narrative pacing were developed through shared storyboards exchanged between Blender, Unreal Engine, and Reaper. The final movie combines prerecorded musical performances with spatial audio renderings synchronized to the camera motion, offering viewers a journey through the evolving architecture, acoustics, and musical practices of Notre-Dame across the centuries.
