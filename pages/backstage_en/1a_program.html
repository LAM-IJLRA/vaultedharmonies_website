Below is a list of all musical works featured in the original movie. To accommodate the shorter formats, the 360° version includes pieces 1, 6, 9, and 11, while the VR version includes pieces 1, 6, and 11 only.

1. Léonin (1150–1210): Organum Iudea et Ierusalem. Ensemble Pérotin le Grand. Sylvain Dieudonné, conductor.

2. Pérotin (1160–1230): Viderunt Omnes (Christmas season). Ensemble Pérotin le Grand. Sylvain Dieudonné, conductor.

3. Philippe de Vitry (1291–1361): Adesto (motet excerpt from the Robertsbridge Codes (14th century), piece for organ. Christophe d’Alessandro, organist.

4. Walter Frye (?–1474): Ave regina caelorum, piece for organ. Christophe d’Alessandro, organist.

5. Antoine Brumel (1460–1515): Ave Maria gratia Dei plena. Centre de musique baroque de Versailles (CMBV). Sylvain Dieudonné, conductor.

6. Mathieu Sohier (1500–1560): Kyrie from the Mass Vidi speciosam. Centre de musique baroque de Versailles (CMBV). Sylvain Dieudonné, conductor.

7. Charles Racquet (1497–1664): Fantaisie, piece for organ. Olivier Latry, organist.

8. Henri Frémart (1595–1651): Credo from the Missa eripe me domine. Centre de musique baroque de Versailles (CMBV). Fabien Armengaud, conductor.

9. François-Joseph Gossec (1734–1829): Hymn to Liberty (1792). Vocal class of Catherine Simonpietri. Catherine Simonpietri, conductor.

10. Jean-François Lesueur (1760–1837): Overture of the March of the Coronation. Orchestre des Lauréats du Conservatoire (O.L.C.). Simon Clausse, conductor.

11. Louis Vierne (1870–1937): Westminster Chimes, piece for organ. Olivier Latry, organist.
